CONSUMER PROTECTION ACT

Right to safety
Consumers must be protected against goods and services that are hazardous to life and property.

Right to information
Consumers have the right to know product quality, quantity, price, ingredients, and manufacturer details before purchase.

Right to choose
Consumers have access to a variety of goods and services at competitive prices without unfair restrictions.

Right to redressal
Consumers can seek compensation for defective goods, poor service, or unfair trade practices.

Right to be heard
Consumer interests must be considered at appropriate forums when disputes arise.

Defective goods
Any fault, imperfection, or shortcoming in quality, quantity, or performance makes a product defective and eligible for replacement or refund.

Deficiency in service
Any inadequacy or poor quality in service (delivery, repair, etc.) is considered deficiency and is punishable.

Unfair trade practice
False advertising, misleading claims, hidden charges, or deceptive pricing are punishable.

E-commerce consumer protection
Online sellers must provide accurate product details, transparent pricing, return policies, and refund mechanisms.

Delayed delivery
Failure to deliver goods or services within promised time is a deficiency in service.

Refund and cancellation issues
Refusal or delay in refund after product return or cancellation is actionable under consumer law.

Overcharging
Charging more than the printed price (MRP) or agreed price is illegal.

Fake or counterfeit products
Selling fake or duplicate products is punishable under consumer protection laws.

Warranty and guarantee violation
Failure to honor warranty or guarantee commitments is considered deficiency in service.

Product liability
Manufacturers, sellers, and service providers are responsible for harm caused by defective products.

Misleading advertisements
Advertisements that give false promises or exaggerate product features are punishable.

Hidden charges
Charging undisclosed fees or additional costs without informing the consumer is illegal.

Online fraud in purchases
If an online seller takes payment but does not deliver goods, it is considered unfair trade practice and fraud.

Poor after-sales service
Failure to provide promised support or service after purchase is a deficiency in service.

Damage during delivery
If goods are damaged during delivery, the seller or platform is responsible for compensation or replacement.

Consumer complaint filing
Consumers can file complaints in consumer forums or online portals for dispute resolution.

Jurisdiction of consumer courts
Consumers can file complaints in district, state, or national consumer commissions based on claim value.

Unfair contract terms
Contracts that heavily favor sellers and harm consumer rights can be challenged.

Return and replacement rights
Consumers are entitled to return or replace defective or incorrect products within policy terms.

Digital services complaints
Issues with online subscriptions, apps, or digital services fall under consumer protection law.
